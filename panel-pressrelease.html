Electrum releases version 2.0 with two-factor authentication

Electrum is one of the first Bitcoin wallets to use deterministic key generation. Deterministic wallets can be restored from a secret phrase, called the "seed". This protects users against computer failures, and makes the wallet very easy to backup; only the seed phrase needs to be written on paper.

The new release of Electrum introduces multi-signature deterministic wallets, and various extensions that facilitate their use.

Probably the easiest way to use a multi-signature wallet with Electrum is the two-factor authentication service proposed by Trustedcoin . A wallet protected by Trustedcoin will require two passwords in order to spend funds: the usual wallet password, and an additional one-time password, provided by a smartphone application (Google Authenticator). Since this wallet is fully deterministic, it will always be possible to restore the wallet from its seed, even if Trustedcoin's servers stop to be available. This is a unique feature of Electrum, and an important consideration when choosing a cosigning service.

Electrum 2.0 also includes support for hardware wallets, which offer a good protection against malware. Two hardware wallets are supported: the Trezor by Satoshilabs, and the Btchip/HW1 by Ledger.

Finally, an important new feature in version 2.0 is the support for the Payment Protocol (BIP70) with payment requests. Payment requests are easier to use than Bitcoin adresses, and they provide better consumer protection, because a signed request is a proof that a website requested a payment from a consumer.
